Module 3: Crafting Effective Prompts

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the principles of effective prompt design
  • Learn how to structure prompts for different health security tasks
  • Practice creating prompts that generate accurate, relevant information
  • Identify common pitfalls in prompt formulation and how to avoid them

The Art of Prompt Engineering

Prompt engineering is the process of designing input queries that guide AI models to generate the most useful, accurate, and relevant outputs. In health security contexts, well-crafted prompts can make the difference between receiving generic information and getting actionable, insightful guidance.

Key Principles of Effective Prompts

Be Clear and Specific

Clear, specific prompts help the AI understand exactly what you're asking for. Avoid vague or ambiguous language that could be interpreted in multiple ways.

Provide Context

Include relevant background information and context to help the AI generate more accurate and useful responses, especially for specialized health security topics.

Structure Your Request

Organize complex prompts into clear sections or bullet points to help the AI process and respond to each part of your request.

Define the Format

Specify the desired format for the response (e.g., bullet points, table, step-by-step guide) to receive information in the most useful way.

Ask for Reasoning

Request that the AI explain its reasoning or provide justification for recommendations, especially for complex health security decisions.

Iterate and Refine

Treat prompting as an iterative process. If you don't get the desired response, refine your prompt and try again.

Effective vs. Ineffective Prompts

Ineffective Prompt
Tell me about epidemics.
This prompt is too vague, lacks context, and doesn't specify what information about epidemics is needed. It's likely to generate a general overview rather than actionable information.
Effective Prompt
Provide a structured analysis of the key factors health officials should monitor to identify an emerging respiratory epidemic in its early stages. Include specific data sources, surveillance techniques, and early warning indicators. Format your response as a bulleted checklist that could be used in a rapid response protocol.
This prompt clearly defines the topic, specifies the purpose, requests particular types of information, and indicates the desired format for the response.
Ineffective Prompt
How do I respond to mpox?
This prompt is ambiguous (personal or institutional response?), lacks specificity about the context or phase of the outbreak, and doesn't clarify what kind of response is needed.
Effective Prompt
As a regional health coordinator in a country that has just confirmed its first imported case of mpox, I need to develop a comprehensive response plan. Help me create a 7-day action plan that covers: (1) surveillance and case detection protocols, (2) contact tracing procedures, (3) isolation guidelines, (4) public health communication strategy, and (5) vaccine/treatment resource allocation. For each component, provide specific actionable steps backed by WHO recommendations where applicable.
This prompt defines the user's role, provides specific context about the situation, outlines distinct categories of information needed, and specifies that recommendations should align with authoritative guidelines.

Prompt Templates for Health Security Applications

Template 1: Analyzing Health Security Regulations
I need an analysis of [specific health regulation or policy]. Please provide: 1. A summary of the key provisions in plain language 2. The main obligations for [relevant stakeholders] 3. How this compares to [related policies or international standards] 4. Potential implementation challenges in [specific context] 5. Recommendations for effective compliance Present your analysis in a structured format with clear headings and bullet points where appropriate.
Template 2: Developing Response Protocols
Help me develop a response protocol for [specific health security threat] in [specific setting/context]. The protocol should be structured as follows: 1. Initial Assessment Procedures (first 24 hours) 2. Immediate Containment Measures 3. Stakeholder Communication Plan 4. Resource Mobilization Strategy 5. Data Collection and Reporting Requirements 6. Criteria for Escalation/De-escalation For each section, include practical, actionable steps that align with [relevant guidelines/standards]. The protocol should be suitable for [target audience, e.g., field workers, health officials] with [level of expertise].
Template 3: Information Synthesis
I need to understand the current state of knowledge on [specific health security topic]. Please synthesize information on: - Current scientific consensus - Areas of uncertainty or debate - Recent developments (within the past 2-3 years) - Practical implications for [specific context] Focus on information from reliable sources such as peer-reviewed literature, WHO guidance, and official public health agencies. Where appropriate, indicate the strength of evidence behind different claims.

2026 GCSP Course-Specific Prompt Templates

These templates are specifically designed for topics covered in the 2026 GCSP "Addressing Challenges in Global Health Security" course. They integrate with the main course modules and help you prepare for or follow up on course content.

Template 1: Analyzing the 2025 WHO Pandemic Agreement
I need a comprehensive analysis of the 2025 WHO Pandemic Agreement for [COUNTRY/REGION/ORGANIZATION]. Please structure your analysis as follows: 1. **Key Provisions Summary** - Surveillance and early warning obligations - Pathogen Access and Benefit-Sharing (PABS) system - Equitable access to medical countermeasures - Financing mechanisms - Technology transfer provisions 2. **Implementation Requirements** For [COUNTRY/REGION], what specific actions are required to: - Strengthen laboratory and surveillance capacity - Participate in the PABS system - Ensure domestic manufacturing capability - Meet reporting obligations 3. **Challenges and Opportunities** - Identify 3-5 major implementation challenges for [CONTEXT] - Highlight opportunities for regional/international collaboration - Suggest strategies to overcome resource constraints 4. **Timeline and Milestones** - Key deadlines for Member States - Suggested implementation phases - Priority actions for the first year 5. **Integration with IHR (2005) as amended in 2024** - How does the Pandemic Agreement complement the IHR? - Are there overlapping obligations? - How should countries coordinate implementation? Present this in a format suitable for briefing [SENIOR OFFICIALS/TECHNICAL STAFF/POLICY MAKERS]. Note: Consider mentioning that while the agreement was adopted in 2025, it requires ratification by at least 60 countries to enter into force, and the PABS annex must also be adopted.
Tip: After receiving the AI output, always verify key facts against the official WHO Pandemic Agreement text available at who.int
Template 2: Comparing 2024 IHR Amendments with Previous Versions
Conduct a detailed comparison of the International Health Regulations focusing on the 2024 amendments versus the [2005/2014/2022] version. Structure the comparison as follows: 1. **Major Changes in 2024 Amendments** Create a table comparing: - Surveillance and notification requirements - National IHR Focal Point responsibilities - Points of Entry requirements - Core capacity requirements - PHEIC declaration process - Role of Regional Directors For each area, specify: - What changed - Why it changed (rationale from COVID-19 lessons) - Practical implications for Member States 2. **Enhanced Obligations for [SPECIFIC COUNTRY/REGION]** - New surveillance requirements - Increased transparency obligations - Strengthened laboratory network participation - Enhanced preparedness benchmarks 3. **Implementation Challenges** For a [LOW/MIDDLE/HIGH]-income country context: - Resource requirements for compliance - Technical capacity gaps - Timeline for implementation - Support mechanisms available (WHO, partners) 4. **Monitoring and Accountability** - New evaluation mechanisms - Reporting requirements - Consequences for non-compliance - Support for countries facing implementation challenges 5. **Practical Action Plan** Develop a 12-month implementation roadmap for [ORGANIZATION/COUNTRY] that addresses: - Priority amendments requiring immediate action - Capacity building needs - Budget implications - Stakeholder engagement strategy Format this comparison as an executive briefing suitable for [MINISTRY OF HEALTH/NATIONAL IHR FOCAL POINT/EMERGENCY OPERATIONS CENTER].
Tip: Request specific examples of how countries have approached similar implementation challenges, then verify with WHO regional offices or published case studies.
Template 3: H5N1 Risk Assessment Framework
Develop a comprehensive risk assessment framework for highly pathogenic avian influenza (H5N1) in [COUNTRY/REGION]. The assessment should cover: 1. **Hazard Characterization** - Current H5N1 epidemiology (human cases, geographic spread) - Viral characteristics and recent genetic changes - Mammal-to-mammal transmission evidence - Human-to-human transmission potential - Case fatality rate and severity spectrum - Available countermeasures (vaccines, antivirals) 2. **Exposure Assessment** For [COUNTRY/REGION], evaluate: - Poultry farming practices and biosecurity measures - Wild bird migration patterns and surveillance - Occupational exposure risks (farmers, veterinarians, cullers) - Live bird market practices - Human-animal interface hotspots - Population density in high-risk areas 3. **Vulnerability Analysis** - Healthcare system capacity for surge response - Laboratory diagnostic capability - Surveillance system sensitivity - Stockpiles of vaccines and antivirals - Public health workforce preparedness - Communication infrastructure for rapid response 4. **Risk Characterization Matrix** Create a matrix scoring: - Likelihood of H5N1 introduction [LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH] - Likelihood of sustained human transmission [LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH] - Potential public health impact [LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH] - Overall risk level and confidence in assessment 5. **Early Warning Indicators** Define specific triggers that would indicate: - Increased risk of human infection - Possible human-to-human transmission - Need to escalate response measures - When to activate Emergency Operations Center 6. **Risk Mitigation Strategies** Prioritized recommendations for: - Enhanced surveillance (avian and human) - Biosecurity improvements - Pre-pandemic vaccine deployment strategies - Healthcare worker protection protocols - Community risk communication - Cross-border collaboration mechanisms 7. **Scenario Planning** Develop 3 scenarios: - Best case: Sporadic cases, no sustained transmission - Most likely: Limited clusters with some human transmission - Worst case: Sustained human transmission, pandemic potential For each scenario, outline: - Expected evolution timeline - Response requirements - Decision points for escalation 8. **Monitoring and Review Plan** - Frequency of risk assessment updates - Data sources to monitor - Triggers for reassessment - Stakeholders to involve in review process Present this risk assessment in a format compliant with [WHO STRATEGIC TOOLKIT FOR ASSESSING RISKS/NATIONAL RISK ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK]. Context-specific factors to consider for [COUNTRY/REGION]: [Add any specific local factors, such as poultry industry size, previous H5N1 experience, regional collaboration mechanisms, etc.]
Tip: After AI generates the framework, populate it with real data from WHO, FAO, WOAH (World Organisation for Animal Health), and national sources. Use AI again to help analyze the populated data and generate insights.

Practical Tips for Health Security Prompts

Tip Example
Specify your professional context "As a public health official in a low-resource setting..."
Define the scope clearly "Focus specifically on water-borne disease surveillance in urban areas"
Request evidence-based information "Provide recommendations based on peer-reviewed literature and WHO guidelines"
Ask for multiple perspectives "Present both the advantages and limitations of this approach"
Specify the level of technical detail "Explain this in terms suitable for community health workers with basic training"
Request practical applications "Include concrete examples of how this has been implemented successfully"
