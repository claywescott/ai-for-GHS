About the AI for Global Health Security Course

Course Overview

The "AI for Global Health Security" course is a specialized module within the Geneva Centre for Security Policy's (GCSP) "Addressing Challenges in Global Health Security" program. This introductory course is designed to equip health security professionals with the essential knowledge and skills to leverage artificial intelligence tools in their work.

As artificial intelligence technologies become increasingly accessible and powerful, understanding how to effectively use these tools has become a valuable competency for professionals working in global health security. This course provides a foundation in AI concepts, practical skills for using AI tools, and specific applications relevant to health security contexts.

Learning Goals

By the end of this course, participants will be able to:

  • Understand the basics of generative AI and how it can be applied to health security challenges
  • Access and navigate common AI platforms and tools
  • Craft effective prompts to generate useful information and insights
  • Identify specific applications of AI in global health security contexts
  • Recognize the ethical considerations and limitations of AI in health security applications

Target Audience

This course is designed for various professionals working in health security, including:

  • Public health officials and epidemiologists
  • Emergency management and disaster response personnel
  • Health policy analysts and researchers
  • International organization staff working on health security
  • NGO staff involved in health emergency preparedness and response
  • Security sector professionals with health security responsibilities

No prior experience with artificial intelligence or advanced technical knowledge is required. The course is designed to be accessible to all participants regardless of their technical background.

Course Structure

The course consists of four modules:

  1. Introduction to Generative AI - Understanding the basics of AI technology and its capabilities
  2. Getting Started with AI Tools - Accessing and navigating popular AI platforms
  3. Crafting Effective Prompts - Learning to create prompts that generate useful, accurate information
  4. AI Applications in Global Health Security - Exploring specific use cases and applications in health security contexts

Each module includes knowledge checks and practical exercises to reinforce learning. The course culminates in a final assessment that tests comprehension of all modules.

About GCSP

The Geneva Centre for Security Policy (GCSP) is an international foundation established in 1995 to promote peace, security, and international cooperation. The Centre designs and delivers executive education courses for government officials, diplomats, military officers, international civil servants, NGO staff, and business executives on comprehensive international peace and security policy issues.

This course is part of the GCSP's commitment to addressing emerging challenges in global security, including those related to health security and technological change.
