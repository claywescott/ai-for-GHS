Module 2: Getting Started with AI Tools

Learning Objectives

  • Identify and access commonly available AI platforms suitable for health security applications
  • Create accounts and log in to selected AI tools
  • Navigate the basic interfaces of AI assistants
  • Understand privacy and security considerations when using AI tools

Available AI Tools for Health Security Professionals

Several AI platforms are accessible to health security professionals. Each offers unique features and specializations, though all share common generative AI capabilities. Here are some of the most widely used platforms:

AI Platform Key Features Access Type Best For
Claude (Anthropic) Strong reasoning, document analysis, longer context windows Web interface, API, mobile app Complex reasoning tasks, document analysis, policy development
ChatGPT (OpenAI) Wide availability, web search capabilities, plugins Web interface, API, mobile app General-purpose tasks, quick research, prototyping
Gemini (Google) Web search integration, multimodal capabilities Web interface, API, mobile app Research-heavy tasks, image analysis
Specialized Health AI Tools Domain-specific capabilities, regulatory compliance Varies by provider Clinical applications, data analysis with regulatory requirements

We recommend using Claude, Gemini, or ChatGPT for the exercises in this course. They offer free tiers with excellent capabilities for health security applications. For complex work, the paid versions of Claude Opus 4.6 extended, Gemini 3 Pro, and ChatGPT 5.2 thinking extended are the most accurate and capable. While we'll demonstrate using Claude, the skills are transferable to other platforms.

Getting Started with Claude

Step 1: Creating an Account
  1. Visit claude.ai in your web browser
  2. Click "Sign Up" or "Create Account"
  3. Enter your email address
  4. Create a secure password
  5. Verify your email address (check your inbox for a verification link)
  6. Complete any additional verification steps if prompted
Step 2: Logging In
  1. Return to claude.ai
  2. Enter your email address and password
  3. Click "Log In" or "Sign In"
  4. You may need to complete a security verification step
Step 3: Navigating the Interface

Once logged in, you'll see:

  • Chat Area: The main space where you interact with the AI
  • Input Box: Where you type your prompts or questions
  • New Chat Button: To start a fresh conversation
  • History Panel: Shows your previous conversations
  • Settings: Access to account and conversation settings

Privacy and Security Considerations

When using AI tools in health security contexts, consider these important privacy and security factors:

  • Data Privacy: Information shared with public AI tools may be used for model training unless otherwise specified
  • Sensitive Information: Avoid sharing personally identifiable information (PII), protected health information (PHI), or confidential data
  • Tool Selection: Some organizations may have approved tools with stronger privacy guarantees
  • Organizational Policies: Follow your institution's guidelines for AI tool usage
Best Practices

✓ Use generalized examples rather than real case data

✓ Anonymize any data before sharing with AI tools

✓ Check if your organization has enterprise agreements with AI providers that offer enhanced privacy

✓ Be aware of data retention policies of the AI platform you're using

✓ Verify output with authoritative sources before using in critical applications

Considerations for International Collaboration Context

Navigating the Current Global Health Landscape:

The global health security landscape has evolved significantly. With changes in international cooperation frameworks, including shifts in WHO participation by some nations, health security professionals must be mindful of how they use AI tools for international collaboration.

Practical Implications for AI Tool Selection:

  • Data Sovereignty: Be aware that many AI platforms (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini) are operated by US-based companies. Consider how this affects your data handling decisions for sensitive international health information.
  • Tool Diversity: Consider using multiple AI platforms from different jurisdictions for critical analyses to ensure diverse perspectives and reduce dependency on any single provider.
  • Institutional Policies: Your organization may have specific guidance on AI tool usage in light of current geopolitical contexts. Always check with your institution before sharing sensitive information.
  • Alternative Options: For highly sensitive work, consider:
    • Locally-deployed AI models (like Llama) where data remains under your control
    • European or other regionally-based AI services
    • Enterprise agreements with enhanced data sovereignty provisions
  • Verification Practices: When using AI for analyzing international health regulations, agreements, or policies, always verify outputs against official sources from WHO, regional health organizations, and national health authorities.

Best Practices for International Health Security Work:

  • Use AI as an analytical aid, not as a primary source for policy decisions
  • When working on multi-country initiatives, be transparent about which AI tools you're using
  • Maintain human oversight for all outputs that will inform international collaboration
  • Stay informed about your organization's evolving policies on AI tool usage
  • Document your verification process when AI is used for critical analyses
Example: Using AI for International Health Policy Analysis

Scenario: You need to analyze implications of the 2025 WHO Pandemic Agreement for your country.

Recommended Approach:

  1. Use AI (any major platform) to get a preliminary analysis and identify key themes
  2. Verify all factual claims against the official agreement text from WHO
  3. Cross-reference with your country's official position statements
  4. Consult regional health organization guidance
  5. Use AI to help draft communications, but have human experts review
  6. Do not share confidential national health data with public AI platforms

What to Avoid:

  • Treating AI outputs as definitive legal interpretations
  • Sharing sensitive country-specific health security information
  • Relying solely on AI for understanding evolving international frameworks

Remember:

AI tools are powerful aids for analysis, research, and communication. However, in the current international landscape, maintaining awareness of data sovereignty, verification practices, and institutional policies is more important than ever. The goal is to leverage AI effectively while maintaining the highest standards of international cooperation and data responsibility.
